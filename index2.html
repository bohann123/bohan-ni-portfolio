05 / Portfolio Artifact 2

Machine Learning
vs. Deep Learning.

A revised course artifact that demonstrates AI/ML foundations, problem analysis, and professional technical communication.

Title & Objective

Real-World Case Study Comparison

The objective of this artifact was to compare traditional machine learning and deep learning through two real-world applications. The project focused on selecting an appropriate approach for each problem and explaining why the alternative would be less suitable.

Process

  • Reviewed the course lesson on machine learning and deep learning.
  • Selected house-price prediction as a traditional machine-learning example.
  • Selected facial recognition as a deep-learning example.
  • Compared feature extraction, data needs, complexity, and computing requirements.
  • Revised the original class report for a professional portfolio audience.

Value Proposition

Choosing the right AI approach

This artifact shows my ability to connect foundational AI concepts with practical decision-making. It demonstrates that I can evaluate a problem, identify the type of data involved, choose an appropriate modeling approach, and communicate the reasoning clearly to both technical and nontechnical audiences.

Tools

Course materials, Microsoft Word, AI/ML concepts, and real-world case analysis.

Skills

Machine learning, deep learning, critical thinking, technical writing, and problem analysis.

Audience

Instructors, recruiters, technology professionals, and readers learning AI fundamentals.

Machine Learning Example

House Price Prediction

Suitable approach: Traditional machine-learning models such as linear regression or gradient boosting work well because the inputs are structured and meaningful, including square footage, bedrooms, location, and sales history.

Why deep learning is less suitable: A deep neural network would require more data, computing power, and training time while offering limited benefit when the important features are already clearly defined.

Deep Learning Example

Facial Recognition

Suitable approach: Convolutional neural networks can learn edges, textures, shapes, lighting patterns, and facial structure directly from image data without requiring every feature to be manually defined.

Why traditional ML is less suitable: Manually engineered visual features cannot capture the full complexity and variation of real-world facial images as effectively as a deep neural network.

Evidence of Learning

Professional application of foundational AI/ML skills

The completed comparison report serves as evidence of the artifact. It applies course concepts to authentic real-world cases, explains why each approach fits its problem, and presents the analysis in a clear and organized format. The portfolio version was customized by reducing classroom-only language, strengthening the professional framing, and emphasizing the decisions and skills that matter to employers and technology audiences.
